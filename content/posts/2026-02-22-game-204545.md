Ubisoft's CEO, Yves Guillemot, recently revealed that the company is developing multiple new titles in the Assassin's Creed and Far Cry franchises, both for single-player and multiplayer experiences. This announcement comes amid significant restructuring within the company, which has seen the cancellation of several games and layoffs affecting nearly 18% of its Paris headquarters staff. Guillemot emphasized the importance of adapting to a competitive gaming landscape while maintaining high-quality standards in their upcoming releases.

The restructuring also involves the establishment of autonomous creative houses, aimed at fostering innovation and efficiency. Guillemot acknowledged the concerns raised by employees and unions regarding these changes, expressing his commitment to transparency and open dialogue. As Ubisoft navigates this transformative phase, the gaming community eagerly anticipates the next chapters in its beloved franchises.
