Twenty-five years ago, Dr. Mario 64 marked the end of an era for the Nintendo 64, a console that defined a generation of gamers. As one of the last titles released for the system, it encapsulated the charm and multiplayer fun that Nintendo was known for, while also serving as a bridge between the classic 2D gameplay and the emerging 3D landscape of gaming.

Dr. Mario 64 not only brought back beloved characters like Mario and Wario but also introduced a refined puzzle experience that was accessible to both casual and hardcore gamers. Its multiplayer mode allowed friends and family to gather around the console, reinforcing the social aspect of gaming that Nintendo has always championed. This game stands as a testament to Nintendo's ability to innovate while honoring its legacy.
